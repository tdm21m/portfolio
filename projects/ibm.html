Scaling UX inside global enterprise platforms

Redesigning Blueworks Live, IBM's business process management platform, to meet modern enterprise UX standards while maintaining backward compatibility.

Role

UX Designer

Product

Blueworks Live

Timeline

2015 – 2017

Focus

Enterprise UX, Process Design

Overview

Blueworks Live is IBM's cloud-based business process management tool, used by enterprises worldwide to document, analyze, and improve their operations. I joined the team to modernize the user experience while serving existing enterprise customers.

This was my introduction to enterprise software design—learning how to balance innovation with the constraints of legacy systems, compliance requirements, and deeply entrenched user workflows.

Hero image placeholder — 1400 × 800px

Challenge

Enterprise software carries enormous technical and organizational debt. Blueworks Live had evolved over years with features added incrementally, resulting in an inconsistent experience that frustrated new users while preserving workflows that longtime customers depended on.

The challenge was to modernize without disrupting—introducing contemporary UX patterns while ensuring that existing processes and integrations continued to work exactly as expected.

Image placeholder — 680 × 500px

Image placeholder — 680 × 500px

Solution

I led a progressive redesign strategy, updating the interface in phases that allowed users to adapt gradually. We started with the most visible pain points—navigation and the process diagramming canvas—before moving to deeper features.

I also established a component library aligned with IBM's emerging Carbon Design System, ensuring that our improvements would be consistent with the broader IBM product ecosystem.
